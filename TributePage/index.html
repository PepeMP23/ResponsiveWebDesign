Homero Gonzalez Gonzalez, defender of the Monarch Butterflies

Here's a little bit about Homero's life:

Homero Gómez González (1969/1970 – January 2020) was a Mexican environmental activist, agricultural engineer, and politician. He was the manager of El Rosario Monarch Butterfly Preserve, a component of the Monarch Butterfly Biosphere Reserve. Gómez served as municipal president and commissioner of El Rosario, Michoacán.

Gómez González administered a monarch butterfly sanctuary in the municipality of Ocampo, in Michoacán, an area known for the activity of groups dedicated to clandestine logging of the region's forests.

This sanctuary had been opened just months before his death, as part of the environmentalist's activities to combat illegal logging in that area, which is vital for the monarch butterfly.

He was the spokesperson for this reservation; He is known as the most prominent butterfly activist in Mexico.

González was last seen alive on January 13, 2020, attending a meeting in the town of El Soldado.

His family reported him missing the next day. They received phone calls from individuals who said they had him kidnapped, asking for ransom payments, which human rights activist Mayte Cardona said the family paid.

More than two weeks after his disappearance, his body was found in a well for agricultural use in the state of Michoacán, he had a head injury that occurred before death by drowning.
